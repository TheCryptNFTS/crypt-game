# Freelon City — The 16 Sacred Shapes

**Purpose**: The 16 Sacred Shapes are the foundational design system for all citizens in Freelon City. They ensure that rarity and identity are felt through silhouette before any details are read. This is one of the core pillars of the world's visual language.

## Core Principle
"Most large collections fail at silhouette. Different traits, same read."

Freelon City solved this by building citizens around 16 canonical geometric forms.

---

## The 16 Sacred Shapes

### 1. Canonical Freelon Silhouette (The Baseline)
- The standard citizen form.
- Balanced hood and body proportions.
- Most common starting point for traits.

### 2. Aggressive Variant — Deeper Hood Angles
- Sharper, more menacing hood geometry.
- Conveys intensity or military leanings.

### 3. Arrow-Tip Ceremonial Form (Candle-Citizen)
- Tall, elegant, flame-like silhouette.
- Used for ceremonial or high-status citizens.

### 4. Separate Ceremonial Mask
- Distinct mask element that covers the face.
- Adds a layer of ritual or anonymity.

### 5. Hex-Chain Veils
- Chains or veils made of linked hexes hanging from the hood.
- Evokes transmission, connection, or bondage to the signal.

### 6. Full Hex-Mesh Tessellation
- The face is completely covered in repeating hex mesh.
- Very geometric, machine-like or devout feel.

### 7. Translucent Geometric Veil
- A semi-transparent geometric veil over the face.
- Creates mystery and layered depth.

### 8. Structural Geometric Crown
- A built-up crown structure on top of the hood.
- Signals authority or high caste.

### 9. Hex-Shaped Horns
- Horn-like protrusions in hex geometry rising from the crown.
- Aggressive, powerful, or ancient feeling.

### 10. Round Seamless Face — Glowing Void Framed by Gold
- Smooth, featureless round face with a glowing center.
- Framed by gold. Very sacred or otherworldly.

### 11. Tall Pyramidal Hood — No Face
- Extreme vertical hood with no visible face.
- Vertical hex line running down the front.
- Monolithic, mysterious, priestly or void-like.

### 12. Cathedral-Spire Helm with Plague-Mask Cheek Piece
- Tall spire-like helm combined with a plague doctor style cheek element.
- Strong religious or protective connotation.

### 13. Multiple Gold Transmission Antennae
- Several vertical gold antennae rising from the head.
- Strongly tied to signal, transmission, and machine doctrines.

### 14. Angular Sword-Shape Head
- Head shaped like the tip of a blade or sword.
- Single vertical glow strip.
- Warrior or enforcer aesthetic.

### 15. Pure Pointed Cone Helm — Brutalist Warrior Monolith
- Severe, minimalist pointed cone.
- Brutalist and imposing.
- Feels ancient and unyielding.

### 16. High Pointed Gothic Arch — Cathedral Citizen (Rarest)
- The most extreme and rare shape.
- Tall gothic arch form.
- Reserved for the most significant or sacred citizens.

## Usage Guidelines

- These 16 shapes are the **primary rarity and identity layer**.
- When designing new citizens, VFX, UI, or environments, reference these silhouettes first.
- The shapes should feel **sacred and geometric** — never organic unless heavily stylized through the lens of one of the 10 civilizations.

## Next Steps for Production
- Generate clean vector-style reference sheets for each of the 16 shapes.
- Create color and material variations per civilization.
- Build a component library so future assets (cards, VFX, 3D, etc.) stay consistent with these forms.
